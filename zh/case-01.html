Fintech

Market Entry via AIFC

European fintech firm achieves full licensing in Kazakhstan within 90 days through the Astana International Financial Centre.

The Challenge

A fast-growing European fintech company specializing in cross-border payment solutions identified Kazakhstan as a strategic beachhead for Central Asian expansion. However, the firm faced a regulatory landscape it had never encountered: overlapping jurisdictions between the national financial regulator and the AIFC's independent common-law framework, conflicting AML/KYC requirements, and a local banking sector reluctant to onboard foreign digital platforms without established government relationships.

The client's internal legal team had spent six months attempting to navigate the licensing process remotely, only to discover that critical approvals required in-person stakeholder engagement with both the AIFC Authority and the National Bank of Kazakhstan. Meanwhile, two competing fintech firms from Asia were already in advanced stages of their own licensing applications, threatening to capture first-mover advantage in the Kazakh digital payments market.

Time was running out. The client needed a partner who could cut through bureaucratic complexity, align multiple regulatory bodies, and deliver a fully operational license before the competitive window closed.

Our Solution

GCR Consulting deployed a dedicated three-person team to Astana within 72 hours of engagement. Our approach combined regulatory strategy with on-the-ground stakeholder management. We began by conducting a comprehensive gap analysis of the client's existing compliance documentation against AIFC requirements, identifying 14 critical deficiencies that would have triggered application rejection.

Simultaneously, we activated our network of relationships within the AIFC Authority, the Astana Hub innovation ecosystem, and key officials at the National Bank. We arranged a series of structured introductions that positioned the client not merely as another foreign applicant, but as a strategic partner aligned with Kazakhstan's Digital Kazakhstan 2025+ initiative. This reframing was critical: it elevated the application from routine processing to priority consideration.

Our team managed every aspect of the licensing process end-to-end, from capital adequacy documentation and technology infrastructure audits to establishing local banking partnerships with Halyk Bank and Kaspi for payment settlement. We also negotiated a sandbox arrangement that allowed the client to begin limited operations during the final approval phase, effectively compressing the go-to-market timeline.
