Litigation & Crisis Turnaround

When your business is under active threat, we take control of the situation, protect what matters, and find a way through.

Strategic Landscape

Commercial crises here move faster and with less predictability than most Western companies are prepared for. Local courts are not always the right forum for foreign parties. Regulatory actions can escalate before there is time to respond through normal channels.

What makes the difference is not the strength of your legal position - it is the decisions made in the first 72 hours, and whether the person making them understands both the legal and operational dimensions of the crisis at once.

We do. That is why clients call us first.

  • Who it's forShareholders under dispute, executives facing personal legal exposure, and financial institutions holding distressed debt in the GCR
  • When you need itA partner is attempting to dilute your shareholding or seize assets; regulatory or law enforcement action has been initiated; the business is insolvent or approaching insolvency
  • What you getDispute transferred to international arbitration + personal legal protection for executives + crisis management leadership installed + valuable assets preserved through a structured process
  • TimelineEmergency response - within 48-72 hours; full crisis stabilization - 4-12+ weeks depending on the situation

What We Deliver

Core - Crisis Response

01. Complex Dispute Resolution & International Arbitration

When a local court is not the right forum, we move your dispute to international arbitration - and manage the full process through to resolution. Where a negotiated settlement is possible, we pursue it first. Either way, your position is protected.

02. White Collar Defense

When an executive or shareholder faces personal legal exposure, we step in immediately. We manage the process with investigating authorities, protect your rights at every stage, and build a clear defense strategy - so you can focus on the business while we handle the legal front.

03. Restructuring & Controlled Insolvency

Financial distress is a solvable problem when handled early and correctly. We separate what has real value from what doesn't, restructure the business around its strongest assets, and where necessary, manage insolvency in a way that preserves what matters - the customer base, the licenses, the team.

04. Chief Restructuring Officer (CRO)

Sometimes the most valuable thing we can do is put the right person in the room. We install an experienced interim executive to lead operations through the critical period - making clear decisions, maintaining stakeholder confidence, and reporting directly to you until stable leadership is in place.

Post-Crisis Recovery (LTV Options)

Distressed Asset Recovery

Where assets have been improperly transferred or seized during the crisis, we manage the recovery process - through legal enforcement, negotiated return, or structured acquisition at distressed prices.

Business Stabilization

Once the acute crisis is resolved, we manage the transition back to normal operations - rebuilding management capability, restoring stakeholder relationships, and establishing controls that prevent a recurrence.

The Methodology

Step 1: Crisis Assessment

Immediate situation analysis. Primary threat identified, assets and relationships at risk mapped, actions required in the first 72 hours defined.

Step 2: Stabilization

Legal protection activated. CRO installed if required. Immediate threats to assets or personal liability addressed.

Step 3: Resolution

Dispute moved to arbitration or negotiated resolution. Restructuring designed and initiated. Criminal or regulatory defense managed.

Step 4: Recovery

Business stabilized under new governance. Distressed assets recovered where possible. Transition to normal operations managed.

Project Spotlight

Client

Foreign Minority Shareholder of an Oil Refinery (Oil & Gas Downstream)

Context

The majority local partner initiated an additional share issue to dilute the client's share, simultaneously organizing a tax audit to block accounts and force the sale of the share for a pittance.

Solution

Our White Collar Defense team blocked criminal risks for the director appointed by the client. In parallel, we initiated arbitration proceedings in London (LCIA) according to the shareholders' agreement and, using GR resources, attracted the attention of the relevant ministry to the conflict.

Result

Attack stopped. A settlement agreement was signed: the client exited the asset (Cash-out) at fair market valuation, preserving reputation and capital.
